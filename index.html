    Frequently Asked Questions

    Q: What is GFRP Rebar made of?

    GFRP (Glass Fiber Reinforced Polymer) Rebar is made of high-strength glass fibers and a durable resin matrix, making it corrosion-resistant and lightweight.

    Q: Where can GFRP Rebar be used?

    It’s ideal for bridges, highways, marine structures, water treatment plants, industrial floors, tunnels, and residential/commercial buildings.

    Q: How strong is GFRP compared to steel?

    GFRP rebars provide higher tensile strength than steel while being 4x lighter, making handling and installation easier.

    Q: Does GFRP Rebar corrode like steel?

    No, GFRP is 100% corrosion-resistant, even in harsh environments like marine or chemical exposure.

    Q: What sizes are available?

    We offer GFRP rebars in standard diameters from 6mm to 32mm, with custom lengths available upon request.

    Q: Is GFRP Rebar cost-effective?

    Yes. While the upfront cost may be slightly higher than steel, its long service life and zero corrosion maintenance make it more economical in the long run.

    Q: Can GFRP Rebar be cut or bent on-site?

    Yes, GFRP rebars can be easily cut with simple tools like a grinder or diamond blade. However, unlike steel, they cannot be bent on-site and should be ordered in required shapes.

    Q: Is GFRP approved for construction projects?

    Absolutely. GFRP Rebar is approved by international standards such as ACI, ASTM, and CSA, and widely used in government, infrastructure, and private projects worldwide.
